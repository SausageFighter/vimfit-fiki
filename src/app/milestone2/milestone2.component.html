Idea Validation

The idea validation is one of the most important stages when creating a start-up. We have questioned more than 240 persons using Google Forms questionnaires (you can find ours here), aged between 14 and 50 years, males and females, certified trainers, and regular persons. We have collected their feedback (here), we had conversations beside the questionnaires, we listened to their stories and hopefully, we understood their necessities, pains, and desires. We merged all the answers in designated tables, for gym-goers and Personal Trainers, that you can find right here.

Conclusions on the survey results

Our users

Non-active and active gym-goers make the most out of our survey with personal trainers coming in at a third of the completion rate.

Motivation factors for our users

As the survey is distributed throughout, we will find out more conclusive data that revolves around health issues and unhealthy habits. Now, none of the survey takers have any health issues though most seem to rely on unhealthy habits to cope with stressful situations, which the pandemic has aggravated in one way or another by making us more sedentary, consume chaotically, dealing with weight gain, and having to deal with stress increase.


All of these negative impacts have made most lose grip on the great habits that they have created and few people still deal with lack of motivation and the thought of having to put in the time and effort back into maintaining a clean diet and to get in shape.


Our app will allow Personal trainers to take charge in stressful situations and gain back the control lost with this pandemic, as 50% of the PT community has been affected negatively, and 49% of them either changed paths or ended their careers altogether.

Determination

Personal trainers have the will power and determination to keep going even after they have hit a hard stop when it comes to their careers. They usually keep going to the gym or continue to work out at home if necessary.


Their drive to be certified as a personal trainer is what sets them apart from average gym-goers.

Features

The most used features of any fitness app would be:

automated training

exercise videos

statistics

voice-over exercises

The most useless features on apps are ones with:

no guiding

notifications & ads

no variations

useless reminders

Average age

Gym goers and Personal Trainers will be one of the initial sources that will spin the wheel when it comes to personal training and services used on our app.


The average age is conclusive as most of the survey takers that are at the beginning of their road when it comes to fitness, seem to revolve around that age bracket (22-23). With personal trainers ranging from 19 to 45 years old and the average age of 27.

Consuming time and money

The average days of gym attendance throughout our study group stays at around 3 days a week with levels starting at Beginner (27.6%), Intermediate (52.9%), and Advanced (13.8%) with goals ranging from, getting skinny, becoming toned, or trying to put on muscle.


The average monthly fitness budget overall revolves around 300 RON with variations ranging from 50 to 1000 RON.

Cost expectations

Personal trainers have a higher tendency to spend more on fitness-related purchases, which makes them have higher financial expectations when it comes to offering services.


Cost expectations for time devoted to creating content and holding online sessions with clients estimate around 30-50 RON per session to thousands per month depending on the time invested.

Online resources used

60% of the gym-goers have used an app for tracking calories, fitness journaling, watching demo videos, or reading up on detailed information often appreciating the ease of use throughout the process. 81% of people looked for an app that brought them guidance from professional athletes.


Personal Trainers seek out apps that not only offer them a database of fitness tools such as tutorials, nutrition guides, statistic profiles, and content creating. They also look for apps that allow them to customize everything that helps them or their customers.

Identifying the problem

Personal Trainers and Gym goers are constantly using old technology like spreadsheets, notes, notebooks, messages and pdf files in order to keep track of and maintain a healthy lifestyle. In order to grow and develop, you need to keep track of change and any constant change must be compared to the past in order to distinguish great from good and good from poor.
Let’s take a few examples and begin with powerlifting. There are many newcomers that come to the gym after watching a few videos online about certain exercises, deadlifts and squats for example. They begin to work out not knowing that they have poor form due to lack of building muscle to support bone structure and they end up hurting themselves or even worse causing permanent injury.
The same theory applies when it comes to dieting and healthy eating. People end up causing permanent damage to their bodies trying to lose those extra pounds and after they finish the process, either they end up back to binge eating due to psychological stress or starving himself or herself without knowing the proper education on how to diet.

The solution

We offer all of our prospects a motivator.
A platform that insures a digital medium of communication between professional heath instructors as well as a tool that allows them to keep track of goals, schedules, conversations, documentation, payments, nutrition, personal transformation and much more.
Support is guaranteed each step of the way by certified personnel in each category whether a client needs a personal trainer or a nutritionist. Alternatively, if a client needs to keep track of their goals so that they do not lose sight of why they started their journey in the first place.

Step 1

Focus on the problem that our business is trying to resolve

The fitness industry is constantly growing and more people are looking for a fast transition into the fitness world. Personal trainers are the motivators of each new client to try to help them reach new goals and achieve new potentials.

Personal trainers

Use multiple platforms and try to transition into multiple different accounts to attract clients as well as hold retention.


They establish a relationship with multiple clients monthly to maintain their growth in the industry and the hassle and time lost trying to keep up with new technology is only wasting them not only time, relationships and also money but also the focus and motivation to keep up with their peers.

Gym clients

They look for unattainable shortcuts and use multiple platforms as a motivator to start their gym journey.


However, at some point they are lost and confused along the way, which causes them to lose interest.

Gyms

Acquire expensive platforms to manage their employees that do not offer them an attainable goal setter for each.


We have to keep in mind that everyone is different and having a platform generalize objectives that pro personal trainers have versus a newly certified personal trainers is killing business development as well as wasting precious time and money.

Understanding our industry

Probably the first thing that comes in our mind when we hear “fitness” is a personal trainer who guides a person to exercise. Fitness is a billion of dollars industry and it is more than personal trainers and gyms, now this industry is more complex than ever consisting of exercise DVDs and internet clips, magazines, supplements, doctors, nutritionists, dedicated gym and home equipment, but now a revolution is happening because of the mobile platforms and social media.

Step 7

Building personas

Building buyer personas is a great way to understand the target customers for adopting the right marketing strategy. People have different needs, opinions, pain points, and challenges and we need to understand all of them to get their attention. A buyer persona is a more specific model compared to the target audience, where the general data are used for identification. We have identified these pinpoints from our surveys mostly from fitness influencers on Facebook and Instagram, avid gym-goers as well as family and friends that play with the idea of being/becoming fit.

Step 8

Analyzing and iterating on customer personas

Our main target audience has been classified into two main channels, gym-goers, and personal trainers. These categories are also split into multiple subcategories.

Gym-goers seek ways to improve their fitness by getting guided assistance from professionals whether it is culinary or training advice, at home, in the gym, or outside.

Personal Trainers are split according to their experience and other relevant categories. As they have progressed through our survey we conclude that most of them are looking for ways to better interact with their customers.

Both groups are looking for a way of interacting with each other on a platform that gives them a means of communication as well as tracking performance whether it is from nutritional and training advice to habit coaching. The initial models will be created using the data in the survey. The next iterations will be based on the data collected by the application, starting with the prototype.
